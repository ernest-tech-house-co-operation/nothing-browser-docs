This blog is where I write about both.

## Want Me To Review Your Project?

**The Rules:**

1. **MIT licensed only.** I don't review proprietary stuff. Open source gets open opinions.
2. **You don't mind negative words.** I'm not mean. But I'm not soft.
3. **You don't get mad easily.** If you can't handle someone saying your thing has problems, don't send it.
4. **It has to be a good thing *according to you*.** Not great. Not perfect. Just something you actually stand behind.

**Send your GitHub link to:** `ernesttechouse@gmail.com`

I will review it. I will praise what works. I will call out what doesn't. That's the deal.

---

## What You Will Not See Here

- **Respect for authority.** I don't care if it's from Vercel, Google, or a solo developer in a garage. Code stands or falls.
- **Arguments.** What I write is my view. You see things differently? Good for you. Don't email me about it. It will not make sense at all.
- **Hype.** "10x faster." "The future of X." "Killer feature." No. Show me benchmarks or be quiet.

## Why Bother?

Because most OSS blogs are either:
- **Marketing disguised as reviews**
- **Hype disguised as journalism**
- **Too nice to be useful**

I'm not a YouTuber. I'm not selling a course. I build things. I use things. I write what I see.
